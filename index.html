Research

Research history and statement

My research to date has focused primarily on classical gravitation. Under Dr. Sean Stotyn, I investigated the properties of near horizon geometries for my BSc Honours thesis. Supervised by Dr. Ivan Booth with the mentorship of Dr. Robie A Hennigar, my MSc thesis explored properties of exotic self-intersecting surfaces in black hole interiors. For my PhD, supervised by Dr. Hari Kunduri, I am interested in performing research in the field of quantum gravity, for example tackling questions on gravitational aspects of the AdS/CFT correspondence.

Self-intersecting Marginally Outer-trapped Surfaces (MOTS)

The black hole merger evolution is not fully understood. The process in which two horizons ultimately become one is complicated as the size, shape, and nature of these horizons are dictated by geometry which is evolved nonlinearly with Einstein's general relativity. Recent advancements have made numerically approximating these horizons possible throughout a binary black hole merger simulation.
A marginally outer-trapped surface (MOTS) is a surface of vanishing outward null expansion. The most popular example of a MOTS are apparent horizons, which are often taken as the outer-most MOTS (and is popularly known as the event horizon, although this is seldom the case). With new MOTS-finding techniques, it was found that self-intersecting MOTSs played a key role in the merger process [1903.05626 gr-qc]. Since then, these self-intersecing MOTSs have been found in extremely simple black hole interiors [2005.05350 gr-qc].

The goal of the research is to investigate these self-intersecting surfaces in a variety of scenarios in the scope of analytical spacetime solutions. The questions include "what properties of the spacetime enable such self-intersecting properties", "are there black hole (thermo)dynamics associated with these self-intersections", and "what do the self-intersections imply in the context of the AdS/CFT duality (of self-intersecting MOTSs found in AdS spacetimes)"? Papers towards these questions include [2111.09373 gr-qc, 2210.15685 gr-qc]. Here is a link to an applet made to display some MOTS analysis for the Kruskal-Szekeres case [ link ].

Outreach

I have had the privilege to travel and give talks internationally, both conference presentations (~15 mins) and seminar lectures (~1 hr). A list of upcoming and past appearances with some samples of my public speaking can be found on my CV.
